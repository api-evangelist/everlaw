Independent Journalism + Everlaw Journalism often tells stories on a human scale. A reporter talks to a few people about a specific topic or incident, collates different perspectives and accounts, maybe consults the public record to confirm some basic facts, and produces an article. But sometimes the scale and geography of information that need to be explored to identify the story are literally beyond human capacity.
